Monads have to be conceived as dancing. But the dance is the Baroque dance, in which the dancers are automata; there we have an entire ‘pathos of distance’, like the invisible distance between two monads (space); the meeting between the two of them becomes a parade, or development, of their respective spontaneities insofar as their distance is upheld; actions and reactions give way to a concatenation of postures allotted now and again through distance … . (Deleuze 1993, 78).
