I was doing experimental films in France before I went into the field, when I was 18-20,  and those films were made frame by frame, playing on different kinds of perceptual sensations. For the film I made in Lajamanu in 1979,  I thought to use that technique because of a stupid analogy: I thought the use of time condensation through rhythmic cuts would be like the condensation
in dreams. Dreaming is so important for Aboriginal people, and I thought if we
