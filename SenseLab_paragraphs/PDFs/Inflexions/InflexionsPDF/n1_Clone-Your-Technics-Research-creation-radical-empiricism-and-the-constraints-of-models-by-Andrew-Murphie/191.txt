A vicious radical empiricism perhaps, at the birth of a significant model of behaviour, which should perhaps have warned us about what was to come in the test drive. It may seem strange to ask, in this context, how Pavlov felt about his dogs - about the real relations experienced. About what might have been gained - and lost - had his relations to dogs, his own experience not been subordinated to the pursuit of the model of the conditioned reflex.
