It is not a variation of truth according to the subject, but the conditions in which the truth of a variation appears to the subject” (1993: 20). This truth of a variation is what is discovered by research- creation. Understood this way, all research is research-creation because the real, if you like empirical, world is constantly re-assembling itself, in process. This has been noted by philosophers  from  Alfred  North  Whitehead  to  Gilbert  Simondon,  Isabelle  Stengers  and Gilles Deleuze and Fèlix Guattari. As Brian Massumi notes, even ‘change changes’ (2002:
10).
