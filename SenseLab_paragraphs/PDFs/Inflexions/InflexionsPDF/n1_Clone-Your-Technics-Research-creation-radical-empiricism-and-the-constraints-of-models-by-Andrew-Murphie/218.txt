Heidegger considered the very heart of Kant’s critical project and which ultimately turns on the regulation of the synthesis of time [... at this moment] Kant trots out man’s best friend. Although he needs this dog in order to demonstrate the trick of temporal synthesis that makes any sensible conceptuality possible, it is also clear that he needs to keep this dog on a tight leash; he cannot afford to let it run off or go astray. On  one  reading,  then,  the  Critique  of  Pure  Reason  institutes  a  sort  of  leash  law.
