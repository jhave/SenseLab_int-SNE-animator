Jazz  composer  and   theorist  George   Russell   of  New   England  Conservatory achieved, in his Lydian Chromatic Concept, a conceptual recasting of the "flavors" available to  jazz  musicians (and   which   in  raw,   un-theorized form  had   been available  for   classical   musicians  through  Slonimsky's Thesaurus  of  musical scales),  in order to account both  for  the  more  dense,  and  dissonant harmonies favored by jazz composers, and  the range  of melodic resources available to improvisers in response to any  given  chord.27  In this  landmark work  of music theory, Russell dropped the standard major (Ionian)  and  relative minor  (Aeolian)
