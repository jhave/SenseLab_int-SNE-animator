Ilya Prigogine's crucial  distinction between reversible and  irreversible processes follows  the  critique of duration as  time’s  arrow accomplished by  the  process philosopher Henri   Bergson  (ninety years  earlier).     In  Time  and Free Will  and Matter  and  Memory,  Bergson   critiques the  experience of  duration,  looking to confront the  ideological function  of  the  calculus of  Leibniz   and   Newton,  as manifest in  Immanuel Kant’s  a priori or  transcendental  categories of time  and space.
