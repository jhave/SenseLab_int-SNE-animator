Signs  of neoteny are  clearly  visible  in  humans…Several aspects of the  human body    strongly  remind   zoologists  of   characteristics  typical    among  young, immature, even  embryonic forms  of primates. Among these  are  the  size  of the brain,  which  is very  large  in comparison to the rest of the body  (like an infant’s), the  angle  of head  to spine  (a right  angle),  and  a mostly  hairless body  (Benecke
2002:105). Neoteny is the slowing of somatic  development, epitomized by the amphibian mud puppy Necturus  maculosus, which  retains its larval  appearance
