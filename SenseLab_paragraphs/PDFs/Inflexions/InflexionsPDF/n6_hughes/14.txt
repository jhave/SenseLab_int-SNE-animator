Gins  and  Arakawa argue  that  the  historical construction of the  human as  the singular subjective sovereign ‘I’ inhibits the  body’s ability  to explore  and  know it’s  self. As they  state:  “Terms  such  as ego, consciousness and  psyche, losing  the body   as  they  do,  lack  those  air  passages through  which   the  body   draws  in atmospheric wherewithal” (2002: 2).
