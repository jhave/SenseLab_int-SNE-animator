By  preceding  ‘person’  with   ‘organism’,  Gins  and   Arakawa  de-privilege  the cultural construction of the  ‘I’ that  has  subsumed the  organism from  which  it came;  that  is, they  posit  the  cognitive body  as  a biological  entity  prior  to  the cultural  construction of  it  as  a  person,  the  abstract,  fixed,  teleological (and terminal) subject  that  seeks  to  know  in  reductive, conclusive and  unequivocal terms,  above all else, what  that person ‘is’.
