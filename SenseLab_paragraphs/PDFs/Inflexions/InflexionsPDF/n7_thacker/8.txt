In a sense,  one  of the  main  challenges of post-Kantian philosophy was  to move beyond  this  stalemate,  this  irrevocable  gulf  between  Life  and   the  living.   A solution to the problem was  put  forth  in the form  of a proposition: it is because we as human beings  are both living subjects  thinking about  living objects that we can adequately think  life. In other  words, it is precisely the  collapse  of Kantian critical  distance that   enables us  to  formulate  a  coherent  and   comprehensive ontology of life.
