In March 2008, I went  to a performance of L’Ange de la Mort at Montreal’s Usine C, as part  of the Temps D’Image festival.  Entering into the space, audience members wound their way down a narrow corridor between the black folds of hanging curtains, before emerging into a square room,  each wall (also black curtains) with its own large rectangular screen  suspended in a vertical orientation in the middle of each wall.
