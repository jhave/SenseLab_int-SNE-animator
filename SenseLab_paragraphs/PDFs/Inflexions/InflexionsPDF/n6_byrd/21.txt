Daniel  Hillis  – the  theorist of parallel computing – speaks of a still  higher order of  abstraction:  “over   and   over  again,   at  many   levels…  Functional abstraction  is  what   decouples  the  ideas   from   the  technology…  the  true power of  the  computer is  that  it  is  capable   of  manipulating not  just  the expression of ideas  but  also the  ideas  themselves” (1999: ix-xi). Abstraction decouples ideas  from  the technology, from  the medium, from  writing, from the   Internet,  from   consciousness  and   the   data    site.
