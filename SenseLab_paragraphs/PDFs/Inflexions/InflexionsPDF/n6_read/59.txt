The  ‘water-merchandise  complex’  Barthes   identified  was   a  technology  that formed and  centred everyday things and  ways  of doing  things in a bounded and centred space.  It was  an equipment and  a space  centring porters and  merchants as  subjects,  the  objects  of  merchandise and  the  practices of  their  movement, hoisting and  storage.
