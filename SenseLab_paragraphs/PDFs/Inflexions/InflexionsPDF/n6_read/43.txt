Horizoning and  shielding are respectively the setting  up  of bounds to particular orders and  the setting  up of procedures against disorder. A door  shuts  off the lab as  a  setting   for  experiment;  the  institute locates  and   legitimises  the  lab,  the university supports, legitimises and  emplaces the  institute and  so on. A nested structure appears as  factors  like  academic legitimacy and  public  meaning and significance of the results of the experiment are accounted for.
