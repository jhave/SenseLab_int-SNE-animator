Hence,  Dumas’  emphasis upon felt sensation. Dumas urges  his dancers to ‘chart sensations’ in their bodies  (feel the feelings)  in the context  of dancing. Since the choreographic material does not depend upon given  vocabularies of movement, the dancer must  navigate the reenactment of feelings,  including their novel, hopefully proximate, deviations. How  else to do so but through felt sensation? This work  is not derived from an external look, though it has a look.
