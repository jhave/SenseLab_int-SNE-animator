Like  Keller  or  Arakawa’s  expansive,  atmospheric  sky-of-an-I (“bleu-de-je”  or “aire-de-je”), lines  by Madeline Gins  involve growth and  endless possibilities of expansion through a process  of entanglement or felting rather than  as text-bound elements in a pattern of orthogonal weaving.
