In  the   past   two   decades,  science   and   technology  have   brought  substantial advances in knowledge of human biology,  e.g. mapping of the human genome, and  awareness of the  role  and  significance of genetic  factors  in cancer.  Yet this increase  in knowledge may not be correlated with  increases in life expectancy. In fact, since  1990, technoscience has  been  increasingly exposed as more  likely  to contribute  to  mortality  because  of  the   ways   in  which   it  underwrites  poor consumer  practices  of  health  management,  e.g.   over-eating  and   sedentary lifestyles,  as well  as increased exposure to environmental toxins.
