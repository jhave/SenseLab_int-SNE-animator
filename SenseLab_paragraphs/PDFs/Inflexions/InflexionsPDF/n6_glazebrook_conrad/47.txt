Reversing the  destiny of parenthood means  opening its possibility beyond the strictures and  structures of the nuclear family such that the labour and  tasks, and pleasures and  love of parenting, can be shared. Plato,  or perhaps Socrates,  who himself  had  five children, suggested that  in his ideal  state,  the Republic, children would not  be  parented by  one  woman and  man,  but  rather, according to  the proverb that “among friends everything is common property” (1921: 424a).
