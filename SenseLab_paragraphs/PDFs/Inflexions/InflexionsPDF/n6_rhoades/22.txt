Composition No.  12 with  Blue holds  the  viewers’  attention just  long  enough to generate visually resonant images:  images  that  emerge from the jittery rhythm of the flickering intersections. The painting does not produce a singular static image but  rather generates what  Erin  Manning would describe as “a felt rhythm that invents itself in the watching” (2009: 188). The images  viewers see emerging from Composition No.  12 with  Blue, as  a  frenetically felt  rhythm, only  occur  in  the encounter with  the  painting. This  experienced rhythm is a dynamic movement felt in the  seeing.  Neither the  viewers nor  the  painting produce these  rhythmic images   alone.
