In this manifold, multicolored architecture whose  floors are undulant, with  a spherical shaped room,  a circular tatami room,  etc., a truly  exciting  experiment has just begun to reverse the flow of human life from birth  to death. French Philosopher, Jean-François Lyotard (1924-1998) wrote to his old friends, Arakawa
& Gins  at  the  occasion  of their  Exhibition at  the  Guggenheim Museum, New
York: “Could the body  be younger at sixty years of age than  at fifteen?”
