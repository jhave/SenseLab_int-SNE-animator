“Any  one instance of existence  involves the notion of other  existences, connected with  it  and  yet  beyond it”  (Whitehead 1938: 9). Bodies  in  the  making not  as humans already existing  but  as perceptions on the cusp  of environmentality, an ecological  becoming. “This  notion of the  environment introduces the  notion of
'more  and  less', and  of multiplicity” (9). Prehending not as perceiving before  the world, but as the push-pull of the withness of worlding.
