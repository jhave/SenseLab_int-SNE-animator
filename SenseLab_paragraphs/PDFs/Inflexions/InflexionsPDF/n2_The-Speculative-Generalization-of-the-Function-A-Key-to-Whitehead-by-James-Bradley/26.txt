The ultimate reason (1967b: 179) of any instance of mapping is universal and infinite mapping activity.  However, universal and infinite  mapping activity,  as the formation of form, maps  to structure itself. Mapping to structure constitutes for Whitehead the primary instance of universal and infinite  many-to-one mapping, and he defines it in an unusual and  controversial way.
Whitehead's interpretation of mapping to structure is conceptualist in respect of universals or what  he calls 'eternal objects', and  stands in the Cambridge tradition of speculative theology along with  the work  of his friends and  contemporaries. [9] Thus
