This capacity to “bracket” the  incredible ontological and  epistemological implications is an  achievement that  requires us  to recognize and  then  dissolve our  incredulity as a condition of inquiry, and  it is vital  to any  consideration of plant  intelligence, an enterprise in which  open  and  even  “empty” mind is called for. Using  the widely accepted definitions of Gardner (1999) and  Tupper's (2002) findings suggest that  experience with  a plant  teacher can  foster  what  Gardner called “existential intelligence.”
