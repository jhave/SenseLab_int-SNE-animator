Crucial  to Jünger's formulation is the ultimate “intertwingling” (Nelson) between plant   and  primate  intelligence: the  elaborate mechanisms of  seduction, those solicitations of  the  “invisible kiss”  described by  Maeterlinck, act  not  only  on insects  but  on thought. Inducing visions  of intoxication and  ecstasy,  activating, amplifying and  altering the interpretations of our  dreams, plants in this analysis become    an   “extended  phenotype”  of   primate  thinking  analogous  to   the outsourcing of angiosperm reproduction to insects.
