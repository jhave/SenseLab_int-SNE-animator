While  Maeterlinck sees “revolt” against destiny in plant  strategies of dispersion and   movement, others   have  noted a  sense  of  completion in  this  “vegetative state.”  In Frederick Haselfoot's translation and  commentary on Dante's Divine Comedy, he writes  “The  plant's soul  is complete while  in a vegetative state;  but the  man's  is, in that  stage,  only  on its way  to completion.” Yet the  20th  century German writer and  botanist Ernst  Jünger  is probably more  representative of the phytopsychic tradition in  his  emphasis on  a plant  agency  articulated through figures  of movement, a “triumphal march  of the plant  through the psyche”:
