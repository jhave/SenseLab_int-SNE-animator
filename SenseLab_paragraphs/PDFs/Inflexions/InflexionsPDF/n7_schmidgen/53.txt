One may note that not only the “taylorist fetishism of speed” (FR 166) contributed to turning the concept  of reflex into a popular element of French  culture by 1950. By way of a remarkable ‘parallel action’ initiated by Anglo- American cybernetics and Russian physiology, the reflex was pushed to leave the clinic and the laboratory during this period. In 1951, Pavlovian psychiatrist Grey Walter demonstrated his artificial  tortoises in the context  of an international cybernetics meeting in Paris. Initially,  these devices  were meant to imitate the movements of animals across space. In a further step they were improved by means  of a circuit Walter  called CORA – Conditioned Reflex Analogue.
