Regarding the question as to who traces, for the historian of science, the border between the library-museum and the library-workshop, Canguilhem offers a clear answer: the present. Quoting Bachelard, he presents his book as shedding light on the “current past”  of a science and not as a quasi-palaeontological study of a scientific project that has long since vanished: “In writing the history of the formation of the reflex concept  for the 17th and 18th centuries, we wanted to contribute to something that, with Bachelard, we call in reference to biology  ‘a recurrent history, a history that is illuminated by the finality  of the present’” (FR
