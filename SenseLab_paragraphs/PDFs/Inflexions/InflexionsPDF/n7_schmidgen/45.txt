The couplings between the concept  of reflex and the exploration instruments were certainly productive for 19th-century physiology. But they could only be a first step toward the proper scientific understanding and explanation of reflex phenomena. Only by overcoming the firm alliance between conceptual and instrumental mechanism did it become  possible to conceive  of
the reflex as an authentic biological  phenomenon.
