As Ato Quayson, in his recent  book Aesthetic Nervousness: Disability and the Crisis of Representation, puts  it, following Simon Baron-Cohen, “In lower  functioning autism the autist  understands almost  no metaphors, so everything is taken  literally” (2007: 152).
