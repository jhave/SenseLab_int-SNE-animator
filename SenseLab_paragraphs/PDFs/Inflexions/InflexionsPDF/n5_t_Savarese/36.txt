Interestingly enough, a study from  2005 seems  to  support something like  this proposition (Koshino  et al. 2005). Using  fMRI technology, the  study found that some  people with  autism remember letters  quite  differently from  neurotypicals. They   rely   much   more   heavily  on   the   right   hemisphere,  which   excels   at processing shapes and  visual  information. In contrast, neurotypicals rely  much more  heavily on the left hemisphere, which  excels at processing language.
