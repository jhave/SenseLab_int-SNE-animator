Mesmoperception—that’s what  philosopher Brian Massumi calls “the medium where inputs from all five senses  meet,  across  subsensate excitation, and  become flesh   together,  tense   and   quivering”  (2002:  61).  Because   the   senses   must cooperate with  each  other—and in fact depend on  each  other  to perform their functions—he refers  to  mesmoperception as  the  “synesthetic sensibility” (Massumi 2002: 62).
