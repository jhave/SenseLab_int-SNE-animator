Significantly, Bissonnette’s syntactical challenges in no way interfere with  insight;  his figures  make  powerful and  imaginative connections in a political  context  that  he is very  much  aware of. There is nothing mechanical or obtuse about  them.   As he says in the commentary on a painting entitled “Aunt Theresa  Rings in Her  Eyes Pizzazz,” “I am seriously past  learning swimming in the shallow end of the pool of language” (Biklen 2005: 181).
