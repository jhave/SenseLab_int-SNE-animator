This awareness of the  coincidence between external and  internal events  loosens the   notion  of  where  oneself   ends   and   the   outside  begins,   perforating  the perceived membrane of one’s limit of self, giving  a felt sense of porosity. In Architectural  Body, the  character Robert  says,  “About kinesthesia … I feel  all porous and  it, kinesthesia, functions as that  which  animates porosity” (Gins and Arakawa 2002: 37).
