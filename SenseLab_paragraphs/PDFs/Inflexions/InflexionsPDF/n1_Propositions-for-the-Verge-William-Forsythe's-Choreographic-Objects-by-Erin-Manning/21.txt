Experience is drawn forth by a pastness of the present. This pastness of the present is specious: it feels like the present even though it is already passing. When we actually perceive this pastness as the present in experience, Alfred NorthWhitehead calls it non-sensuous perception. This does not mean  that we can only perceive through the mirage of what  once was.
