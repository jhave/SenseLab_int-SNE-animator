17   There   must   be  inattributable,  “inhuman  eyes”   (C1  81)  or  “animal  souls” everywhere in matter as so many  dispersed points of view.  Hence  when Leibniz in  the  New  System  also  calls  animal souls  “material souls”,  this  is not  because they  are  not  ideal,  but  because they  belong  or appertain to matter, in the  same sense that “bodies can be not only animal but also animated: not because they act upon souls, but to the extent they belong  to them” (TF 120).
