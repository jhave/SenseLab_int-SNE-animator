Descartes keeps  to “the ‘classical’ conception of the concept” (TF 42) which  holds that between the subject and  the attribute there  is reciprocal inclusion. In the proposition ‘I  think’,  I is  inseparable from  the  clear  and  distinct attribute of thinking and  inversely thinking is what  determines the substance of I. However, since  reciprocal inclusion only  functions in nominal definitions, it subordinates individual being  (‘therefore I am’) to the  generality of its logical  identity. What Descartes fails to prove  is that  the notion ‘cogito’ forms  an immanent expression of the real world, in other  words, that  the essence  of a ‘thinking I’ also involves individual existence.
