If we want  to forge a new  relation between thought and  life, we need  to animate thought, not  paralyze life. This  does  not  mean  that  one  makes  no  distinctions, where the  anexact  is  equivalent to  the  inexact.  “Anexact  and   yet  rigorous,” Deleuze and  Guattari resound, echoing  Edmund Husserl (1987: 407). Making rigorous  distinctions  is   part    and    parcel    of   the   much    larger    process    of experimenting and  encouraging change through speculation.
