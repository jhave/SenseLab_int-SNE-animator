Once  one  recognizes the  baroque qualities of  biological   thought, one  quickly comes to appreciate that  biology  also promotes gothic  qualities as well. Elements of biological  thinking force us to confront the unexpected and  provisional nature of  the   explanations  themselves,  requiring  what   Canguilhem  termed “unpredictable conversions” as  quoted in  the  epigraph.
