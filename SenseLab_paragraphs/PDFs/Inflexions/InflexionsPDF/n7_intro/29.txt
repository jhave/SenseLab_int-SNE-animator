[9] Essentially, Deleuze challenges Leibniz’s insistence that  the law of non- contradiction governs what  is possible in this world, and  argues, instead, that diverging series  based  on a theory of pure  difference— beyond opposition and contradiction – belong  to  the  same  motley  world (Deleuze 1994: 123). In  this view, incompossibilities co-exist and are the condition for compossiblity.
