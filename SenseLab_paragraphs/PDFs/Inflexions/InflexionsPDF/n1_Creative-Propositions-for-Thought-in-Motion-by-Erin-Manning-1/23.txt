“Editing has to do with stretches of time, and the degree of intensity with which these exist, as recorded by the camera; not with abstract symbols... but with the diversity of life perceived” (Tarkovsky 1987: 119). Editing from within plays with thought in motion, emphasizing the rhythmic pulsations that make the film’s inner duration felt. “There is rhythm whenever there is a transcoded passage from one milieu to another, a communication of milieus, coordination between  heterogeneous  space-times.”  (1987:  313).
