In the first video,  Il s’agit (2003, 4’20’’, dancer Ugo Dehaes),  the static torso of
a doll-like  mannequin coincides with  the moving limbs of a dancer who, eyes closed,  head  swinging, fidgets  against a black background. The mechanical fluttering and wriggling movements of the dancer’s arms  (always in dis- accord  with  a musical track of syncopated noises,  and then of fluid  sonic modulations) shatter and multiply the unity  of the image,  before the recomposition of a final meta-static moment.
