We already have  the language for describing this  Wittgensteinian phenomenon. In  the  land   of  semantic  sense,   the  ordinary leaves   out  the  ubiquitous, and Wittgenstein’s return to the ordinary from  Frege’s third realm  is a return to the habitual matrix  of meaning, which  is precisely the  target  of Arakawa and  Gins’ undoing.
