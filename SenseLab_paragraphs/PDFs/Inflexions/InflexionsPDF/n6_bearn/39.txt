Wittgenstein gives,  as an  example of a grammatical joke, Lewis  Carroll’s:  “we called  him  tortoise because he  taught us”  (Baker  and  Hacker 1980: 522-3). The depth of a grammatical joke, Wittgenstein tells us, is lost  if we take  them  to be about  the  arbitrariness of language (Baker  and  Haker 1980: 523). He  suggests rather that what  is deep  about  grammatical jokes is that they reveal  the “limits  of language”  (Wittgenstein 1979: 68).
