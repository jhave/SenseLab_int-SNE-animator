These implications also bleed  into how  one thinks of life and  vitality. Motion  has long been implicated with  life, where the vitality of a living being is judged by its ability  to command movement. Many  nineteenth century theories of life, for instance, privileged motion as not only an important indicator of life (remember that  over  long  time  scales,  even  plants appear to  be  in  constant motion and bacteria engage in simple  directed motions such as chemotaxis), some went  as far as   to   suggest  that   motion  was   a   key   indicator  for   the   development  of consciousness.
