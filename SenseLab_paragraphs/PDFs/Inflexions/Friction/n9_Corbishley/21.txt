For  many  the  gaze  has  been  turned—the preoccupation with  “what’s  over there” has  shifted to  a  noticing of  the  ground under our  feet,  as  well  as corporeal  and   incorporeal  species   of  things  and   things-in-process.  These include: the  air,  animals, flora  and  fauna,  archaeology, geology,  geography, traffic  and   weather. This  shift  is  a  new  type   of  nomadic and   distributed movement  for  this   community  without  reference to  any   known  map   or previously systematized mode  of doing  things.
