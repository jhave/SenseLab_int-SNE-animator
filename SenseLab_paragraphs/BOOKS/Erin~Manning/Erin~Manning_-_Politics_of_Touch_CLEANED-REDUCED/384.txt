Technology alters the manner in which bodies gather information. Can we conceive of touch itself as a technology that is prosthetic to the “organic” body? Since bodies can “survive” without touch, touch could be construed as a tool, a craft, an expressive relation of the body toward the world. Techne, it is useful to remember, refers to a craft or tool that supplements the body’s activities. If we consider the body’s activities to be mere survival mechanisms, we might assume that the organs would count as the extent of the body’s armory. But we know that survival depends on more than our blood pumping. It is in this regard that the senses can be considered as techne, technologies (or prostheses) of the moving body.
