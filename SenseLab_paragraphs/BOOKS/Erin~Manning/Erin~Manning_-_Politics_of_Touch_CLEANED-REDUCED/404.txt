Derrida writes:
First it should be noted that if this freedom ... seems to characterize social and political behaviours, the right and power of each to do what he or she pleases, the faculty of decision and self-determination, as well as the license to play with various possibilities, it presupposes, more radically still, more originally, a freedom of play, an opening of indetermination and indecidability in the very concept of democracy, in the interpretation of the democratic. (2005, 25)
