Perhaps because Michelangelo, the artist who sought more than many others to arouse the human imagination to the sense of touch, displaying in his many sculptures the tactile sensuality essential in modeling the human body—could not conceive of a relationship between God and Adam that didn’t begin with touch. Perhaps because it is time to consider the violence—the eruption of adjacent space-times—created by that touch that has been neglected, the touch necessary for a broader understanding of the manner in which we interrelate and invent the politics by which we live and die.
