Ellen Lupton writes:
The rise of digital media over the past decade has changed the practice of design..., the primacy of the skeleton has given way to the primacy of the skin.... New materials react to light, heat, touch and mechanical stress. Translucency and mutability have replaced transparency and permanence. The outer envelope has detached from the interior volume. Flexible membranes are embedded with digital and mechanical networks.... Industrial skins have assumed a life of their own. (2002, 31)
