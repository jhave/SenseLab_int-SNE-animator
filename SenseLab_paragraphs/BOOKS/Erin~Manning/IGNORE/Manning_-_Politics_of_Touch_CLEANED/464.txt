The body that touches is a body that is always in excess of its-self. Brian Massumi writes: “What if the space of the body is really abstract? What if the body is inseparable from dimensions of lived abstractness that cannot be conceptualized in other than topological terms?” (2002a, 177). Bodies reaching toward are abstract bodies. They are abstract because they space, abstract because incorporeal, touching-toward.
