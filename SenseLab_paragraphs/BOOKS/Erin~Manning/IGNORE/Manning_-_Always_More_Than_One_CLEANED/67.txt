Organization is therefore always also experiential and affective—a fielding of relations. According to Suzanne Langer, this quality of life-living accompanies us through “all the vital processes of life, such as breathing, getting hungry . . . , eliminating, falling asleep . . . , or feeling the coming and going of emotions and thoughts” (qtd. in Stern 1985, 54). We are never without the presence of vitality affects. The associated milieu where the force of lifeliving agitates is first of all a fielding of affective incipiencies.
