This statement of reconciliation, in its acknowledgment of the unspeakable suffering of the First Nations, outlines the responsibility of all Canadians in the forced sequestering of children into Indian Residential Schools and acknowledges the irreparable pain that caused. It also acknowledges the failure of the nation-state to give reparations to the First Nations. In all of these ways, it speaks the truth. But what does this truth do other than restratify the uneven relations between the First Nations and those who have the power to apologize? And whose truth is it?
