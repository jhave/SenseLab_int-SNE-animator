Deleuze’s notion of the movement-image is one way of thinking the whole- ness of movement in conjunction with the becoming-molecular of matter. When movement is conceived as transmutational, it is no longer derivative. A movement-image is no longer the image “of ” something else. Movement no longer foregrounds a part-object—it transmutes the image. Movement as image reveals force not as a discrete element of form but as its operational envelope. Holding force in abeyance, the image foregrounds its experiential nature. This experiential wholeness of movement moving-with and moving-within the image is always at once actual and virtual. It is actual when it is prehended. Otherwise it remains virtual, backgrounded in the imperceptible wholeness of its infini- tude.
