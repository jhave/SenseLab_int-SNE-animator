Conatus is an affirmative existence, a way of creating a more-than that is not added to the mode but is part of its technique. To create more-than is to move beyond what it seemed a body could do: to transduce a body toward pure plastic rhythm. This striving is not goal-directed. It is a reaching-toward the capacity to inhabit the almost-virtual of the more-than of experience. This more-than will always be the production of a new actual occasion, the creation of a pure experience out of which a world will emerge. Moving the relation is a striving toward the ineffable experience of the elasticity of the almost.
