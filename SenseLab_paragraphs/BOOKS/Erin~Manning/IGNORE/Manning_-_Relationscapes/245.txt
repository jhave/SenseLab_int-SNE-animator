Affective politics are not moral politics. An emphasis on movement does not promise an emancipatory politics. Affective politics are what we preconsciously make of them. They are of us and with us: we recompose with them. In this way they are much more dangerous and much more powerful than content-driven politics. They are politics for the making. Affect makes bodies even as it is made by bodies. It preempts what a body can do.
