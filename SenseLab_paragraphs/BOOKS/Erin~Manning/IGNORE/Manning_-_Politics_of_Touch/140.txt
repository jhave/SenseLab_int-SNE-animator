What is often left unwritten in renditions of the body (-politic) is the notion of antagonism on which the body rests. This antagonism is a mech- anism of the reproduction and transformation of subjectivity that produces both effect and affect, troubling the political rationalities sustained within the imaginary of the nation-state. Within this matrix of antagonism versus victimization, political enactment stands out as the intersection between local histories mapped onto the template of the body and bodily spaces that challenge the very writing of history (on the body). The multiple subject positions factored by political agency within the vocabulary of state- sovereignty are versions not of a metamorphosing body, but faces of the state.
