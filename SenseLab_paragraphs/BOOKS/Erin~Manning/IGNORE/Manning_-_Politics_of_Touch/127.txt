It is not a question of thinking of the body that touches (as two separate movements) but of the body as touch. If my body is touch, and both touch and movement signal a displacement toward an other, I can begin to con- ceive of my body as that which produces the spaces for its movements of desire. My body spatializes space insofar as my body remains alive to touch. The space inhabited by my body becomes the space of my body. When I touch you, I not only incite you to a reciprocity, I create space with you.
The space that is the body is not a stable, continuous space. It is a space hardened by lack of contact, awakened by the surprise of touch, a space alive with the incompleteness of its spacing (espacement). The body is a space
