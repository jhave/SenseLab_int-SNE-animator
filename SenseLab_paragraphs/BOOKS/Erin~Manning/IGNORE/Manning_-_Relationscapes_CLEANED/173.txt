Moving with movement requires an altered idea of consciousness. This consciousness is not of the body but with the body moving. This is what Feldenkrais and Paxton call “awareness,” a feeling-with of the body moving. This feeling-with is a virtual dance. It is too quick for conscious thought, and yet it composes with it as a layering of felt experience in the making. As José Gil writes: “The immanence of body awareness emerges on the surface of consciousness and henceforth constitutes its essential element” (2006, n.p.).
