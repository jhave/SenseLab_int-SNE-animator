Patterns emerge, and in their emergence they create new kinds of expression in the making, new shapes of enthusiasm in the bodying. As Chammi’s coming-to-words through movement makes clear, language is in the moving. Language moves in the shape of an enthusiasm that lingers precariously at once on the side of anxiety, where there is always worry that communication will prove impossible, and on the side of a kind of overpowering Spinozist joy that undoes language of any pretense of linear representation, redefining what communication can be.
