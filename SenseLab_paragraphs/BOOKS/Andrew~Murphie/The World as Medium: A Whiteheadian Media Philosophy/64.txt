To return to Whitehead, the basic elements of this gathering and movement of differential intensities are in the region of what Whitehead called simply “contrasts” (1978: 228). Contrasts are what unify sense data into actual occasions. They form patterns by which “the sensa are experienced emotionally, and constitute the specific feelings whose intensities sum up into the unity of satisfaction” which is the aim and end of the occasion (1978: 115).
