Moreover in this, as I began to suggest earlier, any syntax or reliable ordering is fluid or transient at best. This is difficult for most theories of perception, cognition, or aesthetics, let alone media and communications theorists and practitioners (think of the syntax of Kant’s sublime for example, with the triumph of reason over imagination as a kind of punctuating full stop, or of the often challenged syntax of Shannon and Weaver’s mathematical model of communication, or think of the forced syntaxes of models of learning with their objectives and outcomes, or of political process and decision making). There is no universal or permanent syntax, either to language or experience. As syntax is indeed an attempt to order language, expression and power, this is a powerful realisation.
