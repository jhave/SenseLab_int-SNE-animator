The first SenseLab event grew from a challenge arising from discussions with Isabelle Stengers,16 who expressed as  a criterion for  her participation in an academic event that it be just that: an event. In  our conversations with Stengers, it became clear that for an “event” to be an event, it is necessary that a collective thinking process be enacted that can give rise to new thoughts through the interaction on site.  It is equally important that potential for what might occur not be pre-reduced to the delivery of already-arrived-at conclusions. The SenseLab took as its challenge to adapt this criteria to research-creation. What makes a research-creation event?
