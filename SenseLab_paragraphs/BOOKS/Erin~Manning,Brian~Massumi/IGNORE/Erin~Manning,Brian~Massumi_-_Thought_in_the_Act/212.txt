This notion of differential embeddedness in the unfolding of a collective, distributed event was explicitly a rejection of the notion of the “common.” Care organized itself not around the common but around the irreducibly singular. It concerned being-different-together and becoming-together as an expression of those differences, as part of a shared process participated in differentially. Care, as care for the event, assumes no commons, in the sense of an equality of access to a preexisting, valorizable resource. It assumes no commonality or ethos of consensus, in the sense of general characteristics or convictions adhered to by all.
