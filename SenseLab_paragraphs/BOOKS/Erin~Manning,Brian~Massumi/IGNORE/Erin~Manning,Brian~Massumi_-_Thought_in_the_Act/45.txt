The equation, it turns out, is much more than two numbers, a plus sign, and an outcome—it is the generating of a fi that modulates experience, that entertains, that busies the body and absorbs attention, that creates a panoply of sense. Mathematics is intrinsically related to the experience of the day’s unfolding, to how the world gyrates with potential, to how time itself works. It is not a discrete tool or task.
