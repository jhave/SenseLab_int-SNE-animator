The Any-Point of Movement
“Where does the movement start and stop?” (Forsythe 2011, Nov. 10). What manner of phrasing is native to movement, in its disjunctively synthesiz- able difference from language and from other registers of experience? This is the core question around which Forsythe organizes his company’s choreo- graphic process and to which he never ceases to return. He has returned to it now, in November 2011, in prerehearsal for the upcoming season. A new piece is to be created from scratch and old ones revived.
