Touch resets relational movement not by bringing it to rest  in this or that human body, but by activating a momentary pause in the frenzy that reorients the field  as a whole.19 Yes, he lays his  hand on her shoulder. Yes, she  stills. But really what is happening is less about him, or about her, or about them touching. It is about giving us a window into the wider field, giving us pause to encounter, with them, the counterpoint of movement and language in a field  of experience continuously folding back on itself.
