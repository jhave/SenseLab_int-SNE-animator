The dancers’ somehow is not the somehow of a practical procedure that is hidden or unapparent. It is transparent. Like when we say that a story is “somehow sad.” The  sadness has  been directly felt.  Just like that, no question. But  all  the same, there is more. The  “somehow” evokes other sadnesses, unspecified. It invokes them into this one, echoing. This  feeling resonates with many another, not fully expressed, in all transparency. It overfills itself with their difference—and the contrast of all sadnesses with happiness. and now, the same moment, supposedly without happiness. He takes her hand. The gesture passes between sadness and happiness, at the speed of a supposition, at the velocity of thought.
