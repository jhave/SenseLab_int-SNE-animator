Organizing bodies does not mean placing bodies on the stage and planning them into forms. It means reorganizing the body itself, in its  commotion with other bodies. It means activating collective rhythm on the level  of a relational movement, a level  on which form does not single out. “When I go into the studio I want to be able  to see what is in front of me, I want to not have another idea.” Idea, here, is allied to form. What-if asks another kind of question, a question of technique: “The biggest challenge, is not seeing what I want to see but seeing what’s actually in front of my eyes” (Forsythe 2003a). A spray.
