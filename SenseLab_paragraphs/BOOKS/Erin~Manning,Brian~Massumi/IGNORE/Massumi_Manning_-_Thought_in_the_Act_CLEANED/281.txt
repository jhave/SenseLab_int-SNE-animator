Thought gathers in the work. It is the event of the work’s unfolding. Not into language, but in painting, on a canvas that seeks  to activate a new way  of  seeing, a new effort at participation. The  intercessor propels  thought, gives  it a place to land, across works. St. John the Baptist’s landing spreads thought  across Ettinger’s canvas, making felt  an unsettling contiguity: “Distance—the face of these lines” (Ettinger 2010). Lines, fielding.
