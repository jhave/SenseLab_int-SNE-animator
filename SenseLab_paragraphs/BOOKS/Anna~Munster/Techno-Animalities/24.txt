Notwithstanding these promising medial directions being opened up by new photographic theory, the self-less selfie under discussion remains resolutely human here. Yet the photographic image is fast becoming a domain in which humanness is challenged. The selfie itself –in spite of its recognizable features of smile, tilted head and outstretched arm – converges with a range of other networked image-making practices such as the photobomb and drone cams.
