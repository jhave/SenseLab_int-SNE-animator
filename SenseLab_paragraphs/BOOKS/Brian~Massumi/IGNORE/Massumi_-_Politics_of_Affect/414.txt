Count them as you wish, but there will always be more
– a surplus or remainder. At either end, in the incipience of event and in its final characterization, there is a more- than – what Deleuze calls ‘extra-being’. This gives a pronounced asymmetry to the world that keeps it lively. It keeps it off balance. The balance-sheet of becoming is always off kilter. The final tally is always an open question. Because of this, the world is always capable of surprising.
