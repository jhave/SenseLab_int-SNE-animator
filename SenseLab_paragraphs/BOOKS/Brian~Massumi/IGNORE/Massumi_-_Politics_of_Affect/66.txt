Brian Massumi: Ethics in this sense is completely situ- ational. It’s completely pragmatic. And it happens be- tween people, in the social gaps. There is no intrinsic good or evil. The ethical value of an action is what it brings out in the situation, for its transformation, how it breaks sociality open. Ethics is about how we inhabit uncertainty, together. It’s not about judging each other right or wrong. For Nietzsche, like Spinoza, there is still a distinction between good and bad even if there’s not one between good and evil.
