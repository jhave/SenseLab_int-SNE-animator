“And the art is not the better or the worse.” The conventional “language” of dance, for Cunningham, has nothing to do with it as art. The aesthetic force of its event is elsewhere. But given the insistence of content to return, and its budding already on the level of the local signs from which the dance-as-dance lifts off, how can the “art” be effectively segregated from its conventional language? Does not the aesthetic question reside precisely in the tension between them?
