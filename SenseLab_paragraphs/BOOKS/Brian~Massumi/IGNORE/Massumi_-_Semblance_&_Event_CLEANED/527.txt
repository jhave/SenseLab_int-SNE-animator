Second Movement: Life Unlimited
9. This is meant also to be taken as a critique of the principle of “openness through closure” upon which the theory of autopoiesis is founded. The critique is that autopoiesis recurs to a theory of formal constitution (albeit of a nonrepresentational variety) that is at philosophical odds with its ambition to theorize lived, and living, genesis. What it lacks, for an effective theory of lived genesis, is a principle of nonformal (qualitative-relational) experience as immediately ingredient in every operation of the world’s constitution. This could only be a principle of feeling (perceptual feeling) understood as primary in relation to logical form, as well as any notion of objective ordering it might  authorize or guarantee.
