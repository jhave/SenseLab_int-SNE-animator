Strenuous critiques, on grounds of anthropomorphism, are sometimes leveled against approaches, like the one advanced here, that affirm the mutual inclusion of the human and nonhuman forms of life on the same continuum of animal life. The charge is that this kind of approach necessarily falls into the anthropomorphic trap of projecting human characteristics on nonhuman animals, most especially when the continuum is also understood in terms of a mutual inclusion of consciousness and life.
