Life in all its dimensions pertains to the transindividual, never to the individual considered separately. It is in the element of the transindividual that life extends itself, proceeding by qualitative blocks taken up in a process of continuous variation, sweeping everything up together in a dynamic unity of mutual inclusion, while at the same time dispersing the unity into a multiplicity of simultaneously contrasting variants that come singularly to mark each step along the way, no sooner to be swept back up into variation. “Universal Tendency” (Deleuze and Guattari 1987, 407): life pulling itself forward through the surpassing of any putatively fixed, individualizable given; relational propulsion through the given, to the emergence of the new.
