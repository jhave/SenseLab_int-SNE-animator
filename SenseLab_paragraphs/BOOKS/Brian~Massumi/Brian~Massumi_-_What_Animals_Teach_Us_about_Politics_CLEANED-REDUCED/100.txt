It follows from Proposition 2 that the political animal does not recognize any rigid opposition between the frivolous and the serious, which is to say, between the enthusiastic expenditure of creative energies and the anchor of function and utility. It nourishes itself on the productive paradox of their processual alliance. Nonnormative ethico-aesthetics resists, with bursts of supernormal propulsion, the leaden demands, so frequently heard, that one’s actions be “relevant” at all cost and that they “contribute to society” in a way that is already recognizable. The animal politics of education seriously needs to play on such demands.
