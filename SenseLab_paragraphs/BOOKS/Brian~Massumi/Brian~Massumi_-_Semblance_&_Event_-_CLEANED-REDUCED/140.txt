What perspective painting does is tap into the abstraction already at the basis of object perception, and carry it to a higher power, where the object itself, and not only touchings of it and movings-around it, are abstracted, that is to say, really appear virtually, in pure appearance. That pure appearance occurs through an actual object—the canvas, frame, and pigment setup. But the painting as actual object in its own right disappears into the abstraction it taps. When you are experiencing painted depth, you aren’t looking at a canvas, you are seeing a scene. You’re seeing through the canvas
