The ‘autonomy of affect’ refers to the separation of the individual from the social. The autonomy of affect refers to the process by which the excess of potential that presses for expression is remaindered after every determinate taking-form, returning to in-form a next expression. The autonomy is of this process. The autonomy of affect is of the turnover of potential on itself, towards the proliferation of ongoing variations on its expression of socially formative force.
