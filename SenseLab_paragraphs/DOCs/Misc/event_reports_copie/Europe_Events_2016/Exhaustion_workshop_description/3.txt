It is fashionable to be busy. It is common to be burnt out. Exhaustion is not only a widely shared experience in contemporary societies, but also a starting point for numerous interventions into critical discourse. Books like Hito Steyerl’s Wretched of the Screen, Harney and Moten’s Undercommons, and Pelbart’s Cartography of Exhaustion think exhaustion both as a precarious condition as well as a space of potential. Exhausted bodies perceive and create differently, thus challenging the business-as-usual of the depleting routine.
