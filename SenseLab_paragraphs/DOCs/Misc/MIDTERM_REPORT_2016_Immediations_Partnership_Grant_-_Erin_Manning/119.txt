Led by PhD student and Immediations Collaborator Martha Sempert. This pop-up proposition used the concept of exposure–of feeling as a way of moving--as an opening for more collaborative and speculative thinking. 18 participants.
Twisted Nietzsche
Vienna
Nov 26-29 2015
Led by P.I. Erin Manning and Co-Applicant Brian Massumi. This performance stages issues related to the "Recomposing Experience" Immediations theme concerning the relationship between nonconscious (affective) knowledge and consciousness, and how rhythms of movement articulate movements of thought and vice versa. The performance was filmed in advance and presented at the Festival. 500 participants – general public.
