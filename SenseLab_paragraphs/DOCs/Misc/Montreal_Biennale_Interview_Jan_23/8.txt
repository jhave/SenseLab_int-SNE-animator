Pure/en soi, and yet folded, complicated, doubled by the promise of something else yet to come.  How does the sense of time as an artwork's affect foreground the primacy of relation? This machinic quality, a propensity to connect is less interesting as the subject of a work than as its potential. That is to say, participation is frequently an untimely experience. When a work works with the contemporary as the "withness" of time, the how of its happening doubles the when and disperses it across time. Perception itself is frequently untimely and messy, but our habits of thought return it to a restricted perception. Perhaps, in order to sense futurity one needs to make oneself comfortable in the hot mess called perception.
