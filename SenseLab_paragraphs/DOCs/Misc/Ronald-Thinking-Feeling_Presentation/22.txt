« La perception, la mémoire, l’imagination, l’intelligence, la pensée elle-même n’ont qu’un exercice contingent tant qu’elles s’exercent volontairement : alors ce que nous percevons, nous pourrions aussi bien nous le rappeler, l’imaginer, le concevoir ; et inversement. La perception ne nous donne aucune vérité profonde, ni la mémoire volontaire, ni la pensée volontaire : rien que des vérités possibles. » (Deleuze 1964 : 120-121) 
 
Neither movement, neither thought are naturally given and inherent to a conscious intentional subject: there is, so to speak, no premeditated act of thinking, no meticulously prepared thinking-in-act, indifferent to the formative pressures of what forces to act. Setting thought into motion is a gesture that forces one to realize that, in creation, its existence is, in fact, never the « one » it strives to accomplish. What is impossible is to move from and by one. For, a thought in propulsion is in the relation. Every thinking-feeling is a relational movement. There is no thought that stands alone. To think is to be propelled by what already moves, it is to relate with what already returns, with what surpasses, exceeds the human while accompanying it.
