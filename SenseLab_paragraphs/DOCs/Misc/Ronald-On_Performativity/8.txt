Is sadness the most expedient way to feel about the reality, the very necessity of not knowing, of unwillingly ignoring what a body can perform? Are desolation and misery what the performativity or vivacity of experience inspire us? But what if it only means that we can only know in the making, in the doing, in the crafting of experience per-forming itself? But what if it only means that alive the present is always, unconditionally?
