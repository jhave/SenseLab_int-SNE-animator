How does a “Poethics of Blackness,” Denise Ferreira da Silva's term, connect to neurodiversity, to neurodiverse life? Gesturing toward decolonial ways of living, of writing, a poethics of blackness “announce(s) a whole range of possibilities for knowing, doing, and existing,” writes Ferreira da Silva (2014: 81). What kinds of emergent sociality are invented at this interstice?
