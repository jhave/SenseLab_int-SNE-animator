The first iteration of the Collective Listening sessions occurred during the Undercommons event in June 2015 out a necessity for taking seriously the propositions of Fred Moten and Stefano Harney (authors of the book called The Undercommons: Fugitive Planning & Black Study) of thinking around musical pieces as their own conceptual constellations, experimenting with the collective listening encounters as forms of “study”and moving with the affective forces of musical activations while re-thinking presupposed categories of “noise and music, chatter and knowledge, pain and truth.”
